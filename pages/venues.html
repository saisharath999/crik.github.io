VENUE GUIDE

BRISTOL COUNTY GROUND

Bristol, England
Venue Capacity : 11,000
Established : 1889

The ground came into existence in 1889, and was bought by none other than WG Grace! Since then, Gloucestershire County Cricket Club have called it home for nearly 130 years.

Three matches will be played at the Bristol County Ground during the ICC Cricket World Cup 2019.

Those three fixtures will double the number of Men’s World Cup games the ground has held, following one in 1983 and two in 1999.

CARDIFF WALES STADIUM

Cardiff, England
Venue Capacity : 15,200
Established : 1854

The Cardiff Wales Stadium is no stranger to hosting ICC tournaments, having been a venue for the last two editions of the ICC Champions Trophy, as well as the ICC Cricket World Cup in 1999.

The first county game held at the ground was in 1967, and their first international fixture was in fact the 1999 Cricket World Cup.

At the ICC Men's Cricket World Cup 2019, the Cardiff Wales Stadium – home of Glamorgan County Cricket Club.

COUNTY GROUND TAUNTON

Taunton, England
Venue Capacity : 8,000
Established : 1882

It has been in use since 1882, and staged one game in the 1983 World Cup and two in 1999, including India’s clash with Sri Lanka, where they racked up a huge 373/6.

Three ICC Cricket World Cup 2019 fixtures will be held at the County Ground Taunton, home of Somerset County Cricket Club.

Overlooked by the towers of three churches, the County Ground Taunton is one renowned for its character and history.

THE RIVERSIDE DURHAM

Durham, England
Venue Capacity : 14,000
Established : 1995

First established in 1995, The Riverside Durham has previously hosted four ICC Cricket World Cup fixtures, all of which took place at the 1999 edition.

England's most northerly ground, the home of Durham Cricket, will host three fixtures at the ICC Men’s Cricket World Cup 2019.

At the ICC Cricket World Cup 2019, Durham will host three fixtures, including England v New Zealand and it will be the home for two Sri Lanka games.

HAMPSHIRE BOWL

Southampton, England
Venue Capacity : 17,000
Established : 2001

The Hampshire Bowl, home of Hampshire County Cricket Club, was opened in 2001 and sits on the outskirts of Southampton on the south coast of England.

The Hampshire Bowl will host ICC Cricket World Cup fixtures for the first time in 2019, with five matches taking place at the venue.

It has hosted international fixtures in all three formats and was also home to five fixtures in the 2004 edition of the ICC Champions Trophy.

HEADINGLEY

Leeds, England
Venue Capacity : 18,350
Established : 1890

Headingley will once again play host to some of the world’s best cricketers this summer, having been part of the 1975, 1979, 1983 and 1999 editions of the ICC Cricket World Cup.

It will host four matches at the ICC Men’s Cricket World Cup 2019, including England v Sri Lanka, and will be where Afghanistan play two of their round-robin fixtures.

The Windies will also appear at Headingley, where they claimed a heroic Test win against England in 2017.

TRENT BRIDGE

Nottingham, England
Venue Capacity : 17,000
Established : 1841

First opened in 1841, Trent Bridge is a truly historic cricket ground. It first hosted a one-day international in 1974 and has been used as a Cricket World Cup venue for the 1975, 1979, 1983 and 1999 editions.

At the ICC Cricket World Cup 2019 it will host five matches, including England v Pakistan.

Trent Bridge, which is the home of Nottinghamshire - was the venue where England set a record ODI score of 481/6, with Alex Hales and Jonny Bairstow both making centuries.

THE OVAL

London, England
Venue Capacity : 25,000
Established : 1845

The Oval will host five fixtures at the ICC Cricket World Cup 2019, including the opener between England and South Africa.

It has also played host to a number of big World Cup matches, with ten fixtures taking place at the Oval across the 1975, 1979, 1983 and 1999 editions.

The most recent Cricket World Cup fixture at the Oval saw Pakistan spinner Saqlain Mushtaq take a hat-trick against Zimbabwe in 1999.

SEMI-FINAL

EDGBA STON

Birmingham, England
Venue Capacity : 24,500
Established : 1886

Allan Donald and Lance Klusener were the men at the crease that day, with a mix-up in the middle when the scores were level meaning Australia progressed to the World Cup final.

It has been a venue at four previous ICC Cricket World Cups, and was a semi-final location in the 1979, 1983 and 1999 editions.

It will host five games at the ICC Cricket World Cup 2019, including one semi-final and the high-profile meeting of England and India.

OLD TRAFFORD

Manchester, England
Venue Capacity : 24,600
Established : 1857

The ground itself is no stranger to World Cup cricket having been used in the 1975, 1979, 1983 and 1999 editions of the tournament.

It also will be the venue for the most-watched fixture in international cricket when India take on Pakistan.

Old Trafford, home of Lancashire County Cricket Club, will be the busiest venue at the ICC Men’s Cricket World Cup 2019, as it will play host to six games, including a semi-final.

FINAL

LOR D'S

London, England
Venue Capacity : 28,500
Established : 1814

First opened in 1814 and home to international cricket since 1884, Lord’s has hosted 10 ICC Cricket World Cup fixtures, including the finals of the 1975, 1979, 1983 and 1999 editions.

At the ICC Cricket World Cup 2019, the Home of Cricket will host five fixtures, and will once again be the venue for the World Cup Final.

The highest World Cup total at Lord’s was hit on the first occasion it hosted a fixture in the tournament, with England scoring an impressive 334/4 against India back in 1975.
